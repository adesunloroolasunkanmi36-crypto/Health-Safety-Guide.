Emergency SOS

Your Lifeline in Critical Situations

üî¥ How to Activate Emergency SOS

1
Press and Hold Buttons
Simultaneously press and hold the Side button (on the right edge) and either Volume button (on the left edge) for a few seconds.
2
Wait for the Slider
An Emergency SOS slider will appear on your screen along with options to power off or show your Medical ID.
3
Drag to Call
Drag the Emergency SOS slider to immediately call local emergency services (911 in the US, 999 in UK, etc.).
4
Automatic Countdown Option
If you continue holding the buttons instead of using the slider, your iPhone will begin a countdown and automatically call emergency services when it reaches zero. A loud warning sound will play during the countdown.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important

After an emergency call ends, your iPhone will automatically send a text message to your emergency contacts with your current location. It will continue to send updates if your location changes.

üì° Emergency SOS via Satellite

One of the iPhone 17's most revolutionary features is the ability to contact emergency services even when you have no cellular or Wi-Fi connection. This can be lifesaving in remote areas, during natural disasters, or when infrastructure fails.

üõ∞Ô∏è When Satellite Connection Activates

Emergency SOS via satellite automatically becomes available when:

  • You have no cellular signal
  • You have no Wi-Fi connection
  • You attempt to call emergency services
  • You have a clear view of the sky (outdoors)
1
Initiate Emergency Call
Try to call emergency services normally using Emergency SOS. If no cellular connection is available, your iPhone will prompt you to use satellite.
2
Tap "Text via Satellite"
When prompted, tap the option to contact emergency services via satellite messaging.
3
Answer Quick Questions
You'll answer a few quick questions about your emergency (What happened? Is anyone injured? Are there weapons involved?). This helps emergency responders prepare.
4
Point at Satellite
Your iPhone will show you where to point your phone to connect to a satellite. Hold your phone up and follow the on-screen guidance. You'll need a clear view of the sky‚Äîit won't work indoors or under heavy tree cover.
5
Stay Connected
Once connected, your message will be sent to emergency services. Keep your iPhone pointed at the satellite and stay in the open. Messages can take 15 seconds to several minutes to send depending on conditions.

üí° Pro Tips for Satellite Connection

  • Go outside: Satellite connection requires a clear view of the sky. It won't work indoors, in cars, or under dense forest canopy.
  • Stay still: Movement can interrupt the satellite connection. Once connected, stay in place.
  • Battery matters: Keep your iPhone charged. Satellite connection uses more power than regular cellular.
  • Practice the demo: Go to Settings ‚Üí Emergency SOS ‚Üí Try Demo to practice connecting without actually calling for help.

1

Critical Information for First Responders

üìã What is Medical ID?

Your Medical ID is accessible from your iPhone's lock screen, meaning paramedics and first responders can see your vital medical information without unlocking your phone. This can be life-saving if you're unconscious or unable to communicate.

ü©∫ Medical Conditions

List chronic conditions like diabetes, heart disease, epilepsy, asthma, or any condition that emergency responders need to know about immediately.

üíä Medications

Include all medications you take regularly, including dosages. This prevents dangerous drug interactions during emergency treatment.

Your blood type can speed up emergency transfusions. If you don't know it, ask your doctor at your next visit.

‚öôÔ∏è Creating Your Medical ID

1
Open Health App
Open the Health app on your iPhone.
2
Access Your Profile
Tap your profile picture or initials in the top-right corner.
3
Open Medical ID
Tap Medical ID, then tap Get Started or Edit if you've already created one.
4
Enable Lock Screen Access
Toggle ON "Show When Locked". This is crucial‚Äîwithout this, responders can't access your information.
5
Fill in Personal Information
Add your name, date of birth, photo (helps responders identify you), and any other relevant personal details.
6
Add Medical Conditions
Tap Medical Conditions and list any chronic conditions, diseases, or medical issues. Examples: "Type 2 Diabetes," "Pacemaker," "Epilepsy," "Severe Peanut Allergy."
7
List All Medications
Tap Medications & Supplements and add everything you take regularly. Include prescription medications, over-the-counter drugs, vitamins, and supplements with dosages (e.g., "Metformin 500mg twice daily").
8
Record Allergies & Reactions
Tap Allergies & Reactions and list all known allergies. Be specific: "Penicillin - causes anaphylaxis," "Latex allergy," "Bee sting allergy."
9
Add Blood Type
If you know your blood type (A+, O-, AB+, etc.), add it here. This can be critical for blood transfusions.
10
Include Emergency Notes
Use the Medical Notes field for additional important information: "DNR order on file," "Wears hearing aids," "Speaks only Spanish," "Lives alone."
11
Save Your Medical ID
Tap Done to save all your information.

üí° Medical ID Best Practices

  • Update regularly: Whenever you start or stop a medication, or when your health status changes, update your Medical ID immediately.
  • Be thorough: Too much information is better than too little. Include everything relevant.
  • Use clear language: Write conditions in plain terms that anyone can understand.
  • Test access: Lock your phone and try accessing your Medical ID to ensure "Show When Locked" is working.
  • Tell family: Let your loved ones know you've set this up and where to find it on your lock screen.
